Disc Golf Facts

History

What is Disc Golf? Disc Golf was invented in 1927 throwing tin lids into 4 ft wide circles drawn into sandy patches on school grounds in Canada. Since then it has evolved into throwing plastic discs, each with a different mold to allow for different flights. The goal, similar to regular golf, is to get from the start to a chain link basket is the least amount of throws possible.

Rules

Some basic rules to get you started:

  • Your starting throw must be from the tee pad.
  • Every other throw must be from within a foot behind your lie (Where the disc landed)
  • If you throw out of bounds, unless the course states otherwise, put your lie where the disc last crossed in-bounds
  • You may move your lie up to 3 feet from out of bounds or places specified as obstructions. Otherwise, so matter how rough the area is you play it where it lies
  • You can't step forward if putting within 30 ft of the basket
  • Have Fun!
