Home page

What is Disc Golf? Disc Golf was invented in 1927 throwing tin lids into 4 ft wide circles drawn into sandy patches on school grounds in Canada. Since then it has evolved into throwing plastic discs, each with a different mold to allow for different flights. The goal, similar to regular golf, is to get from the start to a chain link basket is the least amount of throws possible.

Beginner Tips

Every disc will have 4 numbers either on the disc itself or they can be found online. These numbers stand for Speed/Glide/Turn/Fade. Speed defines how fast the disc must be thrown to get the intended flight path. There are 4 types of discs: putter, mid-range, fareway driver, and distance driver. Putters comprise speeds 1-3, mid-ranges are 4-6, fareway drivers are 7-8, and distance drivers are 9-14. Glide is pretty self-explanitory. Turn dictates a discs tendency to go against the natural curve of a throw. Fade dictates how hard the disc will crash after it loses steam.
